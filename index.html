About me

I'm a research assistant at Institute of Computer Vision, Shenzhen Univerisity, working with Prof. Linlin Shen. In the meantime, I'm also a computer vision engineer at Taisau, providing industrial level face recognition algorithms for the company. Previously, I received my Bachelors in Computer Science from Shenzhen University in 2016.

My research interest broadly lies in deep learning, particularly focus on

  • Deep representation learning and its applications on computer vision

  • Convolutional neural network acceleration

  • Interpretability of deep visual representation.

Projects

Deep face representaion for face recognition

I implemented CNN models for high accuracy face recognition, combined with various state of the art approaches, including: age-invariant face representation, deep metric learning, Inception-Residual CNN architecture, joint-bayesian metric, CNN ensemble and domain adaptation. Our applications has been adopted by Shenzhen Government. (report)

Convolutional neural network acceleration

For lowering down the latency of CNN forward computation, I accelerated CNN models by using various approaches, including: depthwise separable convolution, low rank approximation and structural network pruning. We achieved 1.6x to 2.0x speedup ratio depends on network architecture.
